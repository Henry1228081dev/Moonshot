We are building a company that solves invisible groundwater poisoning for 200 million people across 70 countries by predicting which wells are toxic before anyone drinks from them, using a two-phase system: satellite remote sensing that is 86% accurate at zero cost, followed by a $1.50 onsite dip test that pushes accuracy to 98.07%.

2) Problem + Bottleneck
What is the specific, tightly scoped problem you are solving right now?
What is the one biggest constraint, bottleneck, or failure point you are focused on solving first?
Governments cannot afford to test every well—Bangladesh alone has 10 million tube wells, and lab testing at $50–$200 per sample would cost up to $2 billion—so most families drink poison for years undetected. The biggest bottleneck right now is manufacturing our onsite dip test strips to prove they reliably hit 98.07% accuracy in hot, humid field conditions.

3) Why This Is 10x Better
Why is your solution 10x better than the current way this problem is solved?
Traditional screening costs $200 per well and misses 80% of dangerous sites because budgets run out; our Phase 1 satellite scan costs $0, immediately identifies 86% of contaminated wells across an entire country, and our Phase 2 dip test confirms the exact risk at 98.07% accuracy for only $1.50 per flagged well. We cut costs by 200x and time from a few months to a mere hours

4) How It Works (Step-by-Step)

Our system runs in two phases. In Phase 1 (Remote Sensing), we measure 41 parameters from free satellite imagery and public geology maps—things like soil types, slopes, rock formations, and magnetic signatures—without anyone visiting the site. Our computer compares these against 56,952 documented groundwater sites to paint a country-wide risk map; this phase alone is 86% accurate and runs in 10 milliseconds per well. In Phase 2 (Onsite Dip Test), local health workers visit only the 15–20% of wells flagged as high-risk and dip a paper strip that changes color if arsenic, iron, manganese, or acidity is present. A smartphone app reads the colour instantly, pushing the final accuracy to 98.07%. That result feeds back into the map so the system grows smarter every time. From these 46 total inputs, we calculate 73 parameters and identify over 50 substances, resources, and hidden risks, ranging from arsenic and fluoride to illegal mining and lithium deposits.

5) Core Technology + Alternatives
We use satellite data and artificial intelligence to read the land's "fingerprints," plus paper strips that change colour like a pregnancy test. Other teams use expensive lab trucks or simple guessing; satellites let us see the whole country at once without driving there.

6) Prototype Progress
We have built and validated both phases across two completely different environments—the United States at 5.7% contamination and Bangladesh at 41.9% contamination. Phase 1 remote sensing is already achieving 86% accuracy using only zero-cost satellite data. When Phase 2 dip test validation is added, the combined system reaches 98.07% accuracy.

7) Biggest Technical Gap
The gap is the 12.07 percentage point jump between Phase 1 remote sensing at 86% and the full 98.07% certainty needed for public health decisions. We close this by deploying the Phase 2 dip test as the surgical confirmation layer, then feeding every confirmed result back into the satellite model so the 86% baseline climbs higher as more villages are tested.

8) Validation + Learnings
We have spoken to hydrogeologists from BGS, and a professor from Imperial College London, who confirmed that old map models fail because a safe well and a toxic well can sit just 100 meters apart, and to Bangladesh health officials, who said universal testing is financially impossible. 9) Economic Viability
Governments and NGOs pay because they currently waste millions testing safe wells; our system lets them spend that money on filters and clean wells instead. We charge per well screened or sell subscriptions to national water agencies.

10) Why Now
